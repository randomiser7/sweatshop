Fast Fashion

The least fashionable problem



Fast fashion has become a massive problem for many people including the workers who make them, and the environment.

====================================================

What is it?

Fast fashion is the term for inexpensive clothing that is produced rapidly by mass-market retailers in response to the latest fashion trends.

====================================================

Why is it an issue?

Fast fashion is, on the surface, just a problem for the environment. Because of the clothes being thrown away after that trend has passed, increasing the size of landfills and polluting water ways, if not contained. But, it goes even deeper than that. Sweatshops collapse, catch on fire, killing hundreds, thousands even, all because of shabby buildings and ignored health and safety. Cotton farmers dependant on faulty practices- stopping them from making profit, pesticides giving the local children birth defects. That is why it's an issue.

The number of clothes bought since the boom of fast fashion went up 60%

92 Million tonnes of clothing are now discarded yearly

And all that waste has to go somewhere...

====================================================

What can we do about it?

If we want to stop fast fashion, we need to put in the work. Nothing will change if we do nothing about it. For example, you could do what I am doing right now, spread this information so that it's known, make it clear to the companies that you want change, you can help by working for a clothing recycler so that thrown away clothes get reused and not sent to the dump, helping the pollution aspect of it. To help fix these problems, we need to rethink how we think about fashion.
